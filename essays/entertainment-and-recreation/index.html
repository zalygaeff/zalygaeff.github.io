On Entertainment and Recreation 0.1

Proper recreation, procrastination, and some entertainment are needed as much as a proper time management system in order to have a productive and fulfilled life and to be satisfied in general. Therefore, I have outlined a few principles for these activities too.

First and foremost, there should be some time specifically dedicated to the rest. The time when work, pet-projects, education are not allowed at all. Ideally, no laptops or other gadgets involved either. I picked Sunday as the most appropriate day for me (as Saturday being used as a placeholder for all the things I haven’t managed during the workweek). This is the time for reading, going out for a walk, movies and stuff like that.

In addition, I allow myself to take a sick leave when I’m not in a good mood, or just feel like I’m not productive today. Obviously, it doesn’t have to be used as an excuse just to skip work, yet I believe any conscious person is able to evaluate their state of mind and get a day off when appropriate. This isn’t widely spread in our culture, however, it shouldn’t be perceived as something shameful. There’s a simple need for a pause sometimes.

Obviously, when possible the best option is to change the environment, have a journey in other words. Judging from my past this is the most mind-blowing experience possible for recovery and recreation. Ironically, it works in the opposite direction as well: my trips to Asia, for instance, were the most insightful, rich from an ethnographic perspective, and personal growth. Somehow they made me a more confident person and a better professional. However, a trip itself shouldn’t be treated as something that takes a ton of money, time or effort. It may be a simple walk within a district in your city you haven’t explored yet. It works this way too.

Another principle I practice is combining a few activities together. Killing two birds with one stone, basically. So, for example, if I want to make a movie night I’m going to invite some friends I haven’t seen for a while to join. Or I can take a walk around the block just to meditate on a particular thought. If it’s reading, then it should be non-fiction which may somehow improve my life (sci-fi is used as a sleeping pill only). When I play poker or chess I consider them as a cognitive workout, not only as entertainment.

Finally, some hobbies may be a powerful tool for recreation too. The one that works for me is music. I have to admit I’m not quite successful there, yet somehow it gives me an ability to completely switch the focus and leave all the crap behind. It provides me some sort of a meditative state I would even say. There’s nothing easier than launching a piece of software and wearing headphones to leave distractions and worries behind.

So, to sum up, recreation time shouldn’t be underrated as it’s an integral part of a fulfilled life. Yet, it may be used in different ways: sometimes all you need is to netflix and chill to procrastinate, and sometimes there’s an opportunity to get multiple benefits at once.
